    Section 2 Reasoning with Pivots

    A collection of Parsons problems to help students draw conclusions from the shapes of matrices and their pivot positions.

    Exercises Exercises

    1. Span.

    Suppose that \(A\) is a \(5\times3\) matrix. Determine whether it is possible for the columns of \(A\) to span \(\real^p\) for some \(p\) beginning with an observation about the number of rows in the matrix, followed by an observation about the number of pivot positions.
    
\(A\) has 5 rows.
    
---

    
\(A\) has 3 rows.
     #paired
---

    
\(A\) has at most 3 pivot positions.
    
---

    
\(A\) has at most 5 pivot positions.
     #paired
---

    There is a row of \(A\) that does not have a pivot position.
    
---

    Every row of \(A\) has a pivot position.
     #paired
---

    There is a vector \(\bvec\) for which the equation \(A\xvec = \bvec\) is inconsistent.
    
---

    The equation \(A\xvec = \bvec\) is consistent for every vector \(\bvec\text{.}\)

     #paired
---

    The columns of \(A\) do not span \(\real^5\text{.}\)

    
---

    The columns of \(A\) span \(\real^5\text{.}\)

     #paired

    2. Linear independence.

    Suppose \(A\) is a \(3\times5\) matrix. Determine if it is possible that the columns of \(A\) are linearly independent? Begin with an observation about the number of columns of \(A\) followed by an observation about the number of pivot positions.
    
\(A\) has 5 columns.
    
---

    
\(A\) has 3 columns.
     #paired
---

    
\(A\) has at most 3 pivot positions.
    
---

    
\(A\) has at most 5 pivot positions.
     #paired
---

    There is a column of \(A\) that does not have a pivot position.
    
---

    Every column of \(A\) could possibly have a pivot position.
     #paired
---

    The homogeneous equation \(A\xvec=\zerovec\) has infinitely many solutions.
    
---

    The homogeneous equation \(A\xvec=\zerovec\) could have a unique solution.
     #paired
---

    The zero vector \(\zerovec\) can be written as a linear combination of the columns of \(A\) with at least one nonzero weight.
    
---

    It is possible that the only way to express the zero vector \(\zerovec\) as a linear combination of the columns of \(A\) is if all the weights are zero.
     #paired
---

    The columns of \(A\) must be linearly dependent.
    
---

    The columns of \(A\) could be linearly independent.
     #paired
