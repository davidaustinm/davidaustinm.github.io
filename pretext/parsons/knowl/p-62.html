Suppose we have the orthogonal matrix \(Q\) and the diagonal matrix \(D\) where
\begin{equation*} Q = \begin{bmatrix} \frac1{\sqrt2} \amp -\frac1{\sqrt2} \\ \frac1{\sqrt2} \amp \frac1{\sqrt2} \\ \end{bmatrix},~~~ D = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp -1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
and that \(A\) is the \(2\times2\) matrix \(A=QDQ^T\text{.}\) Notice that \(Q\) represents a \(45^\circ\) counterclockwise rotation.
