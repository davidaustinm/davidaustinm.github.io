Exercise 1. The matrix of a linear transformation.

Suppose that \(A\) is a \(2\times2\) matrix and that
\begin{equation*} A\twovec10 = \twovec34,~~~ A\twovec01 = \twovec{-2}1 \end{equation*}
What is the matrix \(A\text{?}\) 
Matrix multiplication constructs linear combinations of the columns of \(A\text{.}\)


---



This means that if the columns of \(A\) are \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{,}\) then



\begin{equation*}
A\twovec{x_1}{x_2} = x_1\vvec_1 + x_2\vvec_2.
\end{equation*}




---



Therefore,



\begin{equation*}
A\twovec10 = 1\vvec_1 + 0 \vvec_2 =
\vvec_1\text{.}
\end{equation*}




---

The first column of \(A\) is then \(\twovec34\text{.}\)


---

Similarly, the second column of \(A\) is \(\twovec{-2}1\text{.}\)


---



The matrix



\begin{equation*}
A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \amp -2 \\
4 \amp 1 \\
\end{bmatrix}\text{.}
\end{equation*}
