Item g.

Sage can be used to find lengths of vectors and their dot products. For instance, if v and w are vectors, then v.norm() gives the length of v and v * w gives \(\vvec\cdot\wvec\text{.}\)

Suppose that

\begin{equation*} \vvec=\fourvec203{-2}, \hspace{24pt} \wvec=\fourvec1{-3}41\text{.} \end{equation*}

Use the Sage cell below to find \(\len{\vvec}\text{,}\) \(\len{\wvec}\text{,}\) \(\vvec\cdot\wvec\text{,}\) and the angle between \(\vvec\) and \(\wvec\text{.}\) You may use arccos to find the angle's measure expressed in radians.
