Activity 1.1.2.

  1. Consider the vectors \(\vvec=\twovec32\) and \(\wvec=\twovec{-1}3\text{.}\) Sketch the vectors using Figure 1.1.6.

  2. Find the lengths \(\len{\vvec}\) and \(\len{\wvec}\) using the dot product.

  3. Find the dot product \(\vvec\cdot\wvec\) and use it to find the angle between \(\vvec\) and \(\wvec\text{.}\)

  4. Consider the vector \(\xvec = \twovec{-2}{3}\text{.}\) Include it in your sketch in Figure 1.1.6 and find the angle between \(\vvec\) and \(\xvec\text{.}\)

  5. If two vectors are perpendicular, what can you say about their dot product?

  6. For what value of \(k\) is the vector \(\twovec2k\) perpendicular to \(\wvec\text{?}\)

  7. Sage can be used to find lengths of vectors and their dot products. For instance, if v and w are vectors, then v.norm() gives the length of v and v * w gives \(\vvec\cdot\wvec\text{.}\)
