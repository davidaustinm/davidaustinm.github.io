Let's begin by studying the matrix \(A=\begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \\ 2 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix} \text{.}\) Of course, this is a symmetric matrix that we know can be written as \(A=QDQ^T\) where

\begin{equation*} D = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp -1 \end{bmatrix},\hspace{24pt} Q = \begin{bmatrix} 1/\sqrt{2} \amp -1/\sqrt{2} \\ 1/\sqrt{2} \amp 1/\sqrt{2} \\ \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
