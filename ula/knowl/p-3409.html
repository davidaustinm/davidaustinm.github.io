Since \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\) are three-dimensional vectors, \(A\) must have three rows. Also, we know that \(\dim~\nul(A) = 2\) and \(\dim~\col(A) = 2\text{.}\) Therefore, the number of columns is \(n=\dim~\nul(A) + \dim~\col(A) = 4\text{.}\) Hence, \(A\) is a \(3\times4\) matrix.
