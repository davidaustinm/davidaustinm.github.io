Item g.

Since \(q_A(\uvec) = q_D(\vvec)\text{,}\) the maximum of \(q_A(\uvec)\) is \(3\text{,}\) which occurs when \(\vvec = Q^T\uvec = \twovec10\text{.}\) This means that \(\uvec=Q\twovec10 = \twovec{1/\sqrt{2}}{1/\sqrt{2}}\text{,}\) the eigenvector of \(A\) associated to \(\lambda=3\text{.}\)

In the same way, the minimum value of \(q_A(\uvec)\) is \(-1\text{,}\) which occurs when \(\uvec=\twovec{1/\sqrt{2}}{1/\sqrt{2}}\text{,}\) the eigenvector of \(A\) associated to \(\lambda=-1\text{.}\)
