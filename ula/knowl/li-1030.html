Item 2.4.5.3.c.

The set of vectors \(\vvec_1,\vvec_2,\ldots,\vvec_n,\bvec\) will also be linearly independent. Since \(A\xvec=\bvec\) is inconsistent, we know that \(\bvec\) cannot be written as a linear combination of the other vectors. We also know that each of the vectors \(\vvec_j\) cannot be written as a linear combination of the others because \(\vvec_1,\vvec_2,\ldots,\vvec_n\) is a linearly independent set.
