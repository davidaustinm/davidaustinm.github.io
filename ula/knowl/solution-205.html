Solution 4.3.5.1.

  1. Multiplying by \(S=\mattwo r00r\) has the effect of multiplying vectors by the scalar \(r\text{.}\) Therefore, if we multiply \(S\) by a matrix, it multiplies all the entries in that matrix by \(r\text{.}\)

  2. The matrix \(S=\mattwo r00r\) has the geometric effect of stretching vectors uniformly by a factor of \(r\) while the matrix \(R=\mattwo{\cos\theta}{-\sin\theta}{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}\) rotates vectors by \(\theta\text{.}\)

  3. We have \(a=b=1\) so we form the matrix \(C=\mattwo1{-1}11 = \mattwo{\sqrt{2}}00{\sqrt{2}} \mattwo{\cos(45^\circ)}{-\sin(45^\circ)} {\sin(45^\circ)}{\cos(45^\circ)}\text{.}\) This shows that \(C\) will stretch vectors by a factor of \(\sqrt2\) while rotating them by \(45^\circ\text{.}\)

  4. Sage will easily verify this relationship.

  5. In the same way, we have \(A^2 = (PCP^{-1})(PCP^{-1}) = PC^2P^{-1}\) and hence \(A^k = PC^kP^{-1}\text{.}\)

  6. We have \(a=1\) and \(b=-1\) so we form the matrix \(C'=\mattwo1{1}{-1}1 = \mattwo{\sqrt{2}}00{\sqrt{2}} \mattwo{\cos(-45^\circ)}{-\sin(-45^\circ)} {\sin(-45^\circ)}{\cos(-45^\circ)}\text{.}\) This shows that \(C\) will stretch vectors by a factor of \(\sqrt2\) while rotating them by \(-45^\circ\text{.}\)
