Solution 2.5.4.1.

  1. If 1000 trucks begin at \(P\text{,}\) that evening we find that 70% of them are at \(P\) with the remaining 30% at \(Q\text{.}\) Therefore, \(T\left(\twovec{1000}{0}\right) = \twovec{700}{300}\text{.}\) Since \(T\left(\twovec{1000}{0}\right) = 1000T\left(\twovec{1}{0}\right)\text{,}\) we see that \(T\left(\twovec{1}{0}\right) = \twovec{0.7}{0.3}\text{.}\)

  2. In the same way, we see that \(T\left(\twovec{0}{1000}\right) = \twovec{500}{500}\) so that \(T\left(\twovec{0}{1}\right) = \twovec{0.5}{0.5}\text{.}\)

  3. The columns of \(A\) are \(T\left(\twovec{1}{0}\right)\) and \(T\left(\twovec{0}{1}\right)\) so that \(A=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 0.7 \amp 0.5 \\ 0.3 \amp 0.5 \\ \end{array}\right] \text{.}\)

  4. Evaluate \(T\left(\twovec{100}{900}\right) = A\twovec{100}{900} = \twovec{520}{480}\text{.}\)

  5. We solve \(T(\xvec) = A\xvec = \twovec{550}{450}\) to find \(\xvec=\twovec{250}{750}\text{.}\)

  6. We denote the distribution of trucks Monday morning by \(\xvec_0 = \twovec{0}{1000}\text{.}\)

    1. Monday evening, we have \(\xvec_1=A\xvec_0 = \twovec{500}{500}\text{.}\)

    2. Tuesday evening, we have \(\xvec_2=A\xvec_1 = \twovec{600}{400}\text{.}\)

    3. Wednesday evening, we have \(\xvec_3=A\xvec_2 = \twovec{620}{380}\text{.}\)

  7. Because \(S(\xvec) = T(T(\xvec)) = AA\xvec = A^2\xvec\text{,}\) the matrix representing \(S\) is \(A^2 = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 0.64 \amp 0.60 \\ 0.36 \amp 0.40 \\ \end{array}\right] \text{.}\)

  8. The matrix is \(A^7 \approx \left[\begin{array}{rr} 0.625 \amp 0.625 \\ 0.375 \amp 0.375 \\ \end{array}\right] \text{.}\)

  9. If we compute \(A^k\xvec_0\) for some very large powers \(k\text{,}\) we find that \(\xvec\approx\twovec{625}{375}\) so there are 625 trucks at location \(P\) and 375 at \(Q\text{.}\)
