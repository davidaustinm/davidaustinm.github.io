Item 3.4.5.10.a.

The columns of \(AR\) are found by multiplying the columns of \(R\) by \(A\text{.}\) If we remember that a vector multiplied by a matrix forms a linear combination of the columns of \(A\text{,}\) we see that the first column of \(AR\) is \(\vvec_1-3\vvec_2\) and that the other two columns are unchanged.

Since \(\det(R) = 1\text{,}\) we have \(\det(AR) = \det(A)\text{,}\) which says that this operation does not change the determinant.
