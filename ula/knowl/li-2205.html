Item c.

We form the matrix \(A\) whose columns are the three given vectors. Expanding along either the second row or third column to take advantage of the zero in the \((2,3)\) entry, we see that \(\det(A) = 0\text{,}\) which means that \(A\) is not invertible. Therefore, the vectors do not form a basis for \(\real^3\text{.}\)
