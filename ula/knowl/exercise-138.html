Exercise 8.

Suppose that \(A\) is an \(n\times n\) matrix.

  1. Assuming that \(\vvec\) is an eigenvector of \(A\) whose associated eigenvector is nonzero, explain why \(\vvec\) is in \(\col(A)\text{.}\)

  2. Assuming that \(\vvec\) is an eigenvector of \(A\) whose associated eigenvector is zero, explain why \(\vvec\) is in \(\nul(A)\text{.}\)

  3. Consider the two special matrices below and find their eigenvectors and associated eigenvalues.

    \begin{equation*} A = \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ 1 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ 1 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right],~~~ B = \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ 2 \amp 2 \amp 2 \\ 3 \amp 3 \amp 3 \\ \end{array}\right]. \end{equation*}
