  1. We see that the vectors \(\overline{\xvec}_k\) do not converge but instead flip between approximations to \(\vvec_2\) and \(-\vvec_2\text{.}\) Also, the multipliers \(m_k\) are converging to \(1\) rather than \(-1\text{.}\)

  2. When applying the power method, the multipliers \(m_k\) are usually formed from the same component of the vectors \(\overline{\xvec}_k\) in every iteration. Here, we see that the multipliers are obtained from the first component, then the second component, then the first, and so on.

  3. Choose one of the components, say, the first one and consider the ratio between that component of \(A\overline{\xvec}_k\) and \(\overline{\xvec}_k\) in place of the multiplier \(m_k\text{.}\) The problem is that you must make sure that this component is not approaching zero.
