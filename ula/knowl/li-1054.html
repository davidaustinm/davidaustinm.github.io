Item 2.4.5.5.b.

There is a nontrivial solution to the homogeneous equation \(A\xvec=\zerovec\text{.}\) Since there is also the trivial solution \(\xvec=\zerovec\text{,}\) there must be infinitely many solutions. This can only happen when there is a column of \(A\) that does not have a pivot position.
