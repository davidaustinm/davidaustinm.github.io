  1. The eigenvalues of \(A^{-1}\) are the reciprocals of the eigenvalues of \(A\text{.}\) They are, therefore, \(-\frac13\text{,}\) \(-5\text{,}\) \(1\text{,}\) and \(\frac14\text{.}\)

  2. If \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\text{,}\) then \(\lambda+7\) is an eigenvalue of \(A+7I\text{.}\) Therefore, the eigenvalues of \(A+7I\) are \(-4\text{,}\) \(6.8\text{,}\) \(8\text{,}\) and \(12\text{.}\)
