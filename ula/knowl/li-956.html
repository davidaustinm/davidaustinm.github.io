Item 2.3.4.11.a.

Finding the reduced row echelon form

\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp 2 \amp 1 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ -2 \amp 0 \amp 2 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp 0 \amp -1 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}

shows that \(\vvec_3\) is a linear combination of \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\span{\vvec_1,\vvec_2,\vvec_3}\) is the plane defined by \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{.}\)
