  1. Working with the \(RGB\) color model, we find that

    1. we produce red with varying degrees of brightness.

    2. we produce blue with varying degrees of brightness.

    3. \(R=G=B=0\text{.}\)

    4. \(R=G=B=255\text{.}\)

  2. Working with the \(YC_bC_r\) color model, we find that

    1. we produce gray with varying degrees of brightness.

    2. we produce blue with varying degrees of brightness.

    3. we produce red with varying degrees of brightness.

    4. \(Y=C_b=C_r = 0\text{.}\)

    5. \(Y=255\) with \(C_b=C_r=0\text{.}\)

  3. If we row reduce the matrix whose columns are the vectors in \(\bcal\text{,}\) we obtain the identity matrix, which means that the vectors are linearly independent and span \(\real^3\text{.}\)

  4. The matrices are

    \begin{equation*} P_{\bcal} = \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1.0000 \amp 0.00000 \amp 1.4020 \\ 1.0000 \amp -0.34413 \amp -0.71414 \\ 1.0000 \amp 1.7720 \amp 0.00000 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}

    and

    \begin{equation*} P_{\bcal}^{-1} = \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 0.29900 \amp 0.58700 \amp 0.11400 \\ -0.16874 \amp -0.33126 \amp 0.50000 \\ 0.50000 \amp -0.41869 \amp -0.081311 \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

  5. To convert from \(RGB\) to \(YC_bC_r\text{,}\) we multiply by \(P_{\bcal}^{-1}\) so that

    1. \(\displaystyle \threevec{Y}{C_b}{C_r} = \threevec{76.2}{-43.0}{127.5}\)

    2. \(\displaystyle \threevec{Y}{C_b}{C_r} = \threevec{29.1}{127.5}{-20.7}\)

    3. \(\displaystyle \threevec{Y}{C_b}{C_r} = \threevec{255}{0}{0}\)

    4. \(\displaystyle \threevec{Y}{C_b}{C_r} = \threevec{0}{0}{0}\)

  6. To convert from \(YC_bC_r\) to \(RGB\text{,}\) we multiply by \(P_{\bcal}\) so that

    1. \(\displaystyle \threevec{R}{G}{B} = \threevec{128}{128}{128}\)

    2. \(\displaystyle \threevec{R}{G}{B} = \threevec{128}{107.4}{234.3}\)

    3. \(\displaystyle \threevec{R}{G}{B} = \threevec{212.1}{85.1}{128}\)

  7. We have

    \begin{equation*} \begin{alignedat}{4} Y \amp {}={} \amp 0.299R \amp {}+{} \amp 0.587G \amp {}+{} \amp 0.114B \\ C_b \amp {}={} \amp -0.169R \amp {}-{} \amp 0.331G \amp {}+{} \amp 0.500B \\ C_r \amp {}={} \amp 0.500R \amp {}-{} \amp 0.419G \amp {}-{} \amp 0.081B\text{.} \\ \end{alignedat} \end{equation*}

    The expression for \(Y\) is a weighted average of the \(R\text{,}\) \(G\text{,}\) and \(B\) values. The expression for \(C_b\) takes half the amount of \(B\) and subtracts the amounts of red and green. Likewise, the expression for \(C_r\) takes half the amount of \(R\) and subtracts the amounts of green and blue.
