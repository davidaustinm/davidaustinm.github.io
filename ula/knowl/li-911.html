Item 2.3.4.1.b.

Let's consider the following vectors.

  1. The equation \(\left[\begin{array}{rrr}\vvec_1 \amp \vvec_2 \amp \vvec_3 \end{array}\right]~\xvec = \bvec\) is consistent so \(\bvec\) is in \(\laspan{\vvec_1,\vvec_2,\vvec_3}\text{.}\)

  2. The vector \(\vvec_3\) is in \(\laspan{\vvec_1,\vvec_2}\) because \(\vvec_3 = \vvec_1-2\vvec_2\text{.}\)

  3. The equation \(\left[\begin{array}{rrr}\vvec_1 \amp \vvec_2 \amp \vvec_3 \end{array}\right]~\xvec = \bvec\) is not consistent so \(\bvec\) is not in \(\laspan{\vvec_1,\vvec_2,\vvec_3}\text{.}\)

  4. \(\laspan{\vvec_1,\vvec_2,\vvec_3}\) is the plane defined by \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{.}\)
