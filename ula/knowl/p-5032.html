  1. If \(\alpha = 0\text{,}\) then \(G'=H_n\) and we see that any Markov chain converges to the steady-state vector \(\qvec\) in one term. That is, \(\xvec_1=\qvec\text{.}\)

  2. After six terms, we have \(\xvec_6=\qvec\) to three digits.

  3. With \(\alpha = 0.5\text{,}\) it is not until \(\xvec_{11}=\qvec\) to three digits. With \(\alpha=0.75\text{,}\) it is not until \(\xvec_{26}\text{.}\)

  4. With \(\alpha=1\text{,}\) we have \(G'=G\) so the Markov chain will never converge.
