We saw that the determinant of a product of matrices equals the product of the determinants; that is, \(\det AB = \det A \det B\text{.}\) Thus far, we have been thinking of the determinant as the area of a parallelogram. We may also think of it as a factor by which areas are scaled under the matrix transformation defined by the matrix. As seen in FigureÂ 3.4.7, applying the transform \(B\) scales the area by a factor of \(\det B\text{.}\) Next applying the transform \(A\) scales the area by a factor of \(\det A\text{.}\) The total scaling is then \(\det A\det B\text{.}\)
