Answer 4.1.4.9.1.

  1. \(\twovec{1}{1}\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda = 1\) and \(\twovec{1}{-1}\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda=-1\text{.}\)

  2. Every two-dimensional vector is an eigenvector with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda=-1\text{.}\)

  3. \(\threevec{0}{1}{0}\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda=1\text{.}\) \(\threevec{1}{0}{0}\) and \(\threevec{0}{0}{1}\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda=-1\text{.}\)

  4. \(\threevec{1}{0}{0}\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda=1\text{.}\)
