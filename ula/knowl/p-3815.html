The multiplicity of each eigenvalue is one so there will be a basis of \(\real^2\) consisting of eigenvectors. In particular, a basis vector for \(E_3\) is \(\twovec12\) and a basis vector for \(E_{-2}\) is \(\twovec{-2}1\text{.}\) This means that \(\left\{\twovec12,\twovec{-2}1\right\}\) is a basis for \(\real^2\) consisting of eigenvectors of \(A\text{.}\)
