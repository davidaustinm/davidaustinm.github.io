Once again, we see that \(\lambda=-2\) is an eigenvalue with multiplicity one and \(\lambda=-3\) is an eigenvalue with multiplicity two. However, \(\dim E_{-3} = 1\) so we are not able to find a basis for \(\real^3\) consisting of eigenvalues of \(A\text{.}\) Therefore, \(A\) is not diagonalizable.
