This means that we may define scalar multiplication and matrix addition operations using the corresponding vector operations.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} a\left[\begin{array}{rrrr} \vvec_1 \amp \vvec_2 \amp \ldots \amp \vvec_n \end{array} \right] {}={} \amp \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} a\vvec_1 \amp a\vvec_2 \amp \ldots \amp a\vvec_n \end{array} \right] \\ \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} \vvec_1 \amp \vvec_2 \amp \ldots \amp \vvec_n \end{array} \right] {}+{} \amp \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} \wvec_1 \amp \wvec_2 \amp \ldots \amp \wvec_n \end{array} \right] \\ {}={} \amp \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} \vvec_1+\wvec_1 \amp \vvec_2+\wvec_2 \amp \ldots \amp \vvec_n+\wvec_n \end{array} \right]. \\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
