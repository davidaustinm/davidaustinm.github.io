1. We see that neither the power method nor the inverse power method converge.

  2. The eigenvalues are \(\lambda=2\) and \(-2\text{.}\) This means that there is not a unique dominant eigenvalue and there is not a unique eigenvalue with the smallest absolute value. The methods try to find first one of them and then the other.

  3. To break the symmetry, we can look for an eigenvalue closest to, say, \(5\text{.}\) When we do this, we find the eigenvalue \(\lambda_1=2\text{.}\) Then look for another eigenvalue closest to \(-5\) to find \(\lambda_2=-2\text{.}\)
