Exercise 3.

Suppose that \(A\) is an \(n\times n\) matrix.

  1. Explain why \(\lambda = 0\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\) if and only if there is a nonzero solution to the homogeneous equation \(A\xvec = 0\text{.}\)

  2. Explain why \(A\) is not invertible if and only if \(\lambda=0\) is an eigenvalue.

  3. If \(\vvec\) is an eigenvector of \(A\) having associated eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{,}\) explain why \(\vvec\) is also an eigenvector of \(A^2\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda^2\text{.}\)

  4. If \(A\) is invertible and \(\vvec\) is eigenvector of \(A\) having associated eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{,}\) explain why \(\vvec\) is also an eigenvector of \(A^{-1}\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda^{-1}\text{.}\)

  5. The matrix \(A=\ \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1 \amp 2 \\ 2 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right]\) has eigenvectors \(\vvec_1=\twovec{1}{1}\) and \(\vvec_2=\twovec{-1}{1}\) and associated eigenvalues \(\lambda_1 = 3\) and \(\lambda=-1\text{.}\) What are some eigenvectors and associated eigenvalues for \(A^5\text{?}\)
