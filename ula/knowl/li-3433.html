Item 5.1.4.10.c.

It is not true that \(\col(A) = \col(L)\text{.}\) Since there is a nonzero vector \(\xvec\) such that \(A\xvec=\zerovec\text{,}\) we know that \(A\) is not invertible and hence its column space \(\col(A)\) is not \(\real^4\text{.}\) However, \(L\) is invertible so we have \(\col(L)=\real^4\text{.}\)
