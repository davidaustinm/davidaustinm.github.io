Solution 3.1.3.1.

  1. Solving these two equations shows that

    \begin{equation*} \bvec_1=\twovec{3}{-1}, \bvec_2=\twovec{-2}{1}\text{.} \end{equation*}

  2. Since the vectors \(\bvec_1\) and \(\bvec_2\) form the columns of \(B\text{,}\) we have \(B=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 3 \amp -2 \\ -1 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right] \text{.}\) We can then verify by multiplying that \(AB=BA=I\text{.}\)

  3. We construct the augmented matrix

    \begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rr|rr} 1 \amp 2 \amp 1 \amp 0 \\ 1 \amp 3 \amp 0 \amp 1 \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rr|rr} 1 \amp 0 \amp 3 \amp -2 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp -1 \amp 1 \end{array}\right]\text{,} \end{equation*}

    which shows that \(A^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{rr} 3 \amp -2 \\ -1 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right] \text{.}\)

  4. Using Sage, we find that \(A^{-1}= \left[\begin{array}{rrr} -\frac{18}{35} \amp -\frac{16}{105} \amp \frac{1}{15} \\ -\frac{12}{35} \amp \frac{1}{105} \amp -\frac{1}{15} \\ \frac{1}{5} \amp \frac{2}{15} \amp \frac{1}{15} \end{array}\right] \text{.}\)

  5. This matrix does not have an inverse because its reduced row echelon form is not the identity \(I\text{.}\)

  6. We find that \((D^{-1}C^{-1})(CD) = I\) so \((CD)^{-1} = D^{-1}C^{-1}\text{.}\)
