Solution 3.1.5.2.1.

  1. \(A=\left[\begin{array}{rr} \frac1{\sqrt{2}} \amp -\frac1{\sqrt{2}} \\ \frac1{\sqrt{2}} \amp \frac1{\sqrt{2}} \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.}\) To undo the rotation, we will perform a \(45^\circ\) clockwise rotation, which is defined by the matrix \(A^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{rr} \frac1{\sqrt{2}} \amp \frac1{\sqrt{2}} \\ -\frac1{\sqrt{2}} \amp \frac1{\sqrt{2}} \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.}\)

  2. \(A=\left[\begin{array}{rr} -1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp -1 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.}\) We see that \(A^2=I\) because rotating by \(180^\circ\) is its own inverse.

  3. We can do this by constructing matrices that define reflections, such as

    \begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp -1 \\ \end{array}\right], \left[\begin{array}{rr} -1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right], \left[\begin{array}{rr} 0 \amp 1 \\ 1 \amp 0 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
