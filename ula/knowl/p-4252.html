Eventually, the vectors become practically indistinguishable from a scalar multiple of \(\vvec_1 = \twovec{2}{1}\text{;}\) that is, \(\xvec_k\approx s\vvec_1\text{.}\) This means that

\begin{equation*} \xvec_k = \twovec{R_k}{S_k} \approx s\twovec{2}{1} \end{equation*}

so that \(R_k/S_k \approx 2\text{.}\) In addition, \(\xvec_{k+1} \approx 1.3\xvec_k\) so that \(R_{k+1}\approx 1.3 R_k\) and \(S_{k+1}\approx 1.3 S_k\text{.}\) We conclude that, after a very long time, the ratio of the populations \(R_k\) to \(S_k\) is very close to 2 to 1. We also see that each population is multiplied by 1.3 every year meaning the annual growth rate for both populations is about 30%.
