  1. Since \(AB\) is invertible, the span of the columns of \(AB\) is \(\real^n\text{,}\) which says that every equation \(AB\xvec = \bvec\) is consistent.

  2. If \(\yvec\) is the solution to the equation \(AB\xvec = \bvec\text{,}\) then \(B\yvec\) satisfies \(A(B\yvec) = \bvec\text{,}\) which means that \(A\xvec = \bvec\) is consistent.

  3. We now know that the span of the columns of \(A\) is \(\real^n\text{,}\) which tells us that \(A\) is invertible.
