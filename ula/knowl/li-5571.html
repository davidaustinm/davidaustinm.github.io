Item 7.4.5.3.b.

In this case, \(G=A^TA = A^2\text{.}\) If \(\lambda_i\geq 0\) are the eigenvalues of \(A\) and \(\vvec_i\) the associated eigenvectors, then \(\lambda_i^2\) is an eigenvalue of \(G\) with associated eigenvector \(\vvec_i\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\sigma_i = \sqrt{\lambda_i^2} = \lambda_i\) and the right singular vectors are \(\vvec_i\text{.}\)

The left singular vectors are \(A\vvec_i = \sigma_i\uvec_i = \lambda_i\vvec_i\) so the left singular vectors are the same as the right singular vectors.
