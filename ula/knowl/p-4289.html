Up to this point, we have focused on \(2\times2\) systems. In fact, the general case is quite similar. As an example, consider a \(3\times3\) system \(\xvec_{k+1}=A\xvec_k\) where the matrix \(A\) has eigenvalues \(\lambda_1 = 0.6\text{,}\) \(\lambda_2 = 0.8\text{,}\) and \(\lambda_3=1.1\text{.}\) The matrix \(A\) therefore has a basis \(\bcal\) consisting of eigenvectors so we can look at the trajectories \(\coords{\xvec_k}{\bcal}\) in the coordinate system defined by \(\bcal\text{.}\) The phase portraits in FigureÂ 4.4.8 show how the trajectories will evolve. We see that all the trajectories will converge into the eigenspace \(E_{1.1}\text{.}\)
