Solution 6.3.4.3.1.

  1. Apply the Projection Formula to find \(\bhat = \fourvec{-1}{-2}13\text{.}\)

  2. \(\bvec^\perp = \fourvec{3}{1}{-7}4\text{.}\)

  3. Constructing a matrix \(A\) whose columns are \(\wvec_1\) and \(\wvec_2\) allows us to find a basis for \(W^\perp=\nul(A^T)\text{.}\) This gives the basis \(\vvec_1=\fourvec{-1}110\) and \(\vvec_2=\fourvec{-1}201\text{.}\) We then have \(\bvec^\perp = -7\vvec_1 + 4\vvec_2\text{.}\)
