  1. \(\left[\begin{array}{rr} 3 \amp -6 \\ -2 \amp 4 \\ \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1 \amp -2 \\ 0 \amp 0 \\ \end{array}\right]\) so \(\bvec\) must be on the line defined by \(\twovec{3}{-2}\text{.}\)

  2. \(\left[\begin{array}{rr} 3 \amp -6 \\ -2 \amp 2 \\ \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right]\) so \(\bvec\) can be any vector in \(\real^2\text{.}\)
