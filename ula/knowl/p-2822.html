We round the Fourier coefficients to the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} 100 \\ -6 \\ 4 \\ 4 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\) and find the approximation \(\yvec\text{,}\) which compares to the original vector \(\xvec\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \yvec=\left[\begin{array}{c} 103 \\ 94 \\ 91 \\ 92 \\ 103 \\ 105 \\ 105 \\ 108 \end{array}\right], \xvec=\left[\begin{array}{c} 103 \\ 94 \\ 91 \\ 92 \\ 103 \\ 105 \\ 105 \\ 108 \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

In this case, the approximation \(\yvec\) is the same as the original vector \(\xvec\text{.}\)
