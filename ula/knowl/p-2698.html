To convert our revenue vectors into the coordinates given by \(\bcal\text{,}\) we form the matrices:

\begin{equation*} P_{\bcal} = \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 1 \amp 1 \amp 1 \amp 0 \\ 1 \amp 1 \amp -1 \amp 0 \\ 1 \amp -1 \amp 0 \amp 1 \\ 1 \amp -1 \amp 0 \amp -1 \\ \end{array}\right],~~~ P_{\bcal}^{-1} = \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} \frac14 \amp \frac14 \amp \frac14 \amp \frac14 \\ \frac14 \amp \frac14 \amp -\frac14 \amp -\frac14 \\ \frac12 \amp -\frac12 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp \frac12 \amp -\frac12 \\ \end{array}\right]. \end{equation*}

In particular, if the revenue vector is \(\xvec = \fourvec{x_1}{x_2}{x_3}{x_4}\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \cfourvec {\frac14 x_1 + \frac14 x_2 + \frac14 x_3 + \frac14 x_4} {\frac14 x_1 + \frac14 x_2 - \frac14 x_3 - \frac14 x_4} {\frac12 x_1 - \frac12 x_2} {\frac12 x_3 - \frac12 x_4}. \end{equation*}

Notice that the first component of \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal}\) is the average of the components of \(\xvec\text{.}\)
