  1. Since \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\) are three-dimensional vectors, \(A\) must have three rows. Also, we know that \(\dim~\nul(A) = 2\) and \(\dim~\col(A) = 2\text{.}\) Therefore, the number of columns is \(n=\dim~\nul(A) + \dim~\col(A) = 4\text{.}\) Hence, \(A\) is a \(3\times4\) matrix.

  2. We can use \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\) as the first two columns of \(A\text{.}\) We also know that \(A\wvec_1=\zerovec\) and \(A\wvec_2=\zerovec\text{.}\) If we call the other two columns \(\vvec_3\) and \(\vvec_4\text{,}\) then \(A\wvec_1=\zerovec\) implies that \(3\vvec_1-\vvec_2+\vvec_3 = \zerovec\) so that \(\vvec_3 = \threevec{-7}{-7}{7}\text{.}\) Since \(A\wvec_2 = \zerovec\text{,}\) we have \(-2\vvec_1+4\vvec_2 + \vvec_4=\zerovec\text{,}\) which says that \(\vvec_4=\threevec{8}{-2}{-18}\text{.}\) This gives

    \begin{equation*} A = \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 2 \amp -1 \amp -7 \amp 8 \\ 3 \amp 2 \amp -7 \amp -2 \\ -1 \amp 4 \amp 7 \amp -18 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
