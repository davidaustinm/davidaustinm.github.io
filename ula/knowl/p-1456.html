We see that

\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rrr|r} 1 \amp 2 \amp 1 \amp a \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \amp b \\ -2 \amp 0 \amp 2 \amp c \\ \end{array}\right] \sim \left[\begin{array}{rrr|r} 1 \amp 2 \amp 1 \amp a \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \amp b \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \amp 2a-4b+c \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

If the equation is consistent, we must have \(2a-4b+c=0\text{.}\) The solution space is the plane defined by \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{.}\)
