Since \(A\) has been diagonized as \(A=PDP^{-1}\text{,}\) the eigenvalues of \(A\) are the diagonal entries of \(D\) and the eigenvectors are the columns of \(P\text{.}\) Therefore, we know the eigenvectors are \(\lambda_1=2\) with associated eigenvector \(\vvec_1=\twovec11\) and \(\lambda_2=-3\) with associated eigenvector \(\vvec_2=\twovec{-1}{2}\text{.}\)

  2. The columns of \(P\text{,}\) \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\text{,}\) form a basis \(\bcal\) for \(\real^2\text{.}\) We find that \(\xvec=\twovec14 = 2\vvec_1+\vvec_2\text{.}\)

  3. Then \(A\xvec=2\lambda_1\vvec_1+\lambda_2\vvec_2= 4\vvec_1-3\vvec_2\text{.}\)

  4. The components of \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal}\) are the weights of the linear combination of \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\) that form \(\xvec\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \twovec21\text{.}\)

  5. In the same way, \(\coords{A\xvec}{\bcal} = \twovec4{-3}\text{.}\)

  6. Each time we multiply by \(A\text{,}\) the vectors \(\vvec_j\) are multiplied by their eigenvalue. This means that \(A^4\xvec=2\cdot 2^4\vvec_1 + (-3)^4\vvec_2 = 32\vvec_1+81\vvec_2\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\coords{A\xvec}{\bcal} = \twovec{32}{81}\text{.}\)
