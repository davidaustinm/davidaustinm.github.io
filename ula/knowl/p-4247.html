Notice that the eigenvectors \(\vvec_1\) and \(\vvec_2\) form a basis \(\bcal\) of \(\real^2\text{.}\) This means that \(A\) is diagonalizable so we can write \(A=PDP^{-1}\) where

\begin{equation*} P = \left[\begin{array}{rr} \vvec_1 \amp \vvec_2 \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 2 \amp -2 \\ 1 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right], \qquad D = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 1.3 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0.5 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

In particular, we have

\begin{equation*} \coords{A\xvec}{\bcal} = D\coords{\xvec}{\bcal}\text{.} \end{equation*}
