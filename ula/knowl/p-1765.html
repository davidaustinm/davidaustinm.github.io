Finally, we wish to compose these two matrix transformations. For instance, if we operate the plants for times given by the vector \(\xvec\text{,}\) we would like to know the required amounts of the ingredients. To determine this, notice that \(T(\xvec) = A\xvec\) tells us how many cakes, donuts, and eclairs we produce. The ingredients required are then given by

\begin{equation*} R(T(\xvec)) = R(A\xvec) = B(A\xvec) = BA\xvec\text{.} \end{equation*}

Notice that the matrix that defines the composition is given by the product of the two matrices defining the matrix transformations.
