Item a.

If \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\text{,}\) then \(A\vvec=\lambda\vvec\) for an associated eigenvector \(\vvec\text{.}\) Multiplying by \(A^{-1}\) and \(\lambda^{-1}\text{,}\) we obtain \(\lambda^{-1}\vvec = A^{-1}\vvec\text{,}\) which shows that \(\lambda^{-1}\) is an eigenvalue of \(A^{-1}\text{.}\)
