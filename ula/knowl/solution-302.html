Solution 6.2.2.1.

  1. We have the equation \(\wvec_1\cdot\xvec = x_1 - 2x_3 = 0\text{.}\)

  2. We have the equation \(\wvec_2\cdot\xvec = x_1+x_2 - x_3 = 0\text{.}\)

  3. These two equations give \(B\xvec=\zerovec\) where

    \begin{equation*} B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \amp -2 \\ 1 \amp 1 \amp -1 \\ \end{bmatrix} \sim \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \amp -2 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ \end{bmatrix}\text{,} \end{equation*}

    whose solutions have the parametric form \(\xvec=x_3\threevec2{-1}1\text{.}\)

  4. By distributivity, \((c_1\wvec_1+c_2\wvec_2)\cdot\xvec= c_1\wvec_1\cdot\xvec + c_2\wvec_2\cdot\xvec = 0\text{.}\)

  5. \(W^\perp\) is the solution space to the equation \(B\xvec=\zerovec\text{.}\) Therefore, a basis consists of the single vector \(\threevec1{-1}1\text{,}\) and \(W^\perp\) is one-dimensional.

  6. Since every vector in \(W^\perp\) is orthogonal to every vector in \(W\text{,}\) the orthogonal complement of \(W^\perp\) is \(W\text{.}\)
