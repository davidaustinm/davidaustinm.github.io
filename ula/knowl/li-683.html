Item 2.2.6.7.c.

We compute that

\begin{equation*} L_1A = \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ -2 \amp 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp 2 \amp -1 \\ 2 \amp 0 \amp 2 \\ -3 \amp 2 \amp 3 \\ \end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp 2 \amp -1 \\ 0 \amp -4 \amp 4 \\ -3 \amp 2 \amp 3 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

To multiply the first row by 3 and add to the third row, we would use \(L_2= \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 0 \\ 3 \amp 0 \amp 1 \\ \end{array}\right] \text{.}\)
