Item 3.5.5.1.d.

We can write a parametric description for the solution space to the homogeneous equation as

\begin{equation*} \xvec = \left[\begin{array}{r} x_1 \\ 2x_4 - 3x_6 \\ -x_4-4x_6 \\ x_4 \\ x_6 \\ x_6 \end{array}\right] = x_1 \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{array}\right] +x_4 \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{array}\right] + x_6 \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

Therefore, a basis for \(\nul(A)\) is

\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{array}\right], \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{array}\right], \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
