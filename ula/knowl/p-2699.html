For our particular revenue vector \(\xvec = \fourvec{10.3}{13.1}{7.5}{8.2}\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = P_{\bcal}^{-1} \xvec = P_{\bcal}^{-1} \fourvec{10.3}{13.1}{7.5}{8.2} = \fourvec{9.775}{1.925}{-1.400}{-0.350}. \end{equation*}

This means that our revenue vector is

\begin{equation*} \xvec = 9.775 \vvec_1 + 1.925 \vvec_2 - 1.400\vvec_3 - 0.350 \vvec_4\text{.} \end{equation*}

We will think about what these terms mean by adding them together one at a time.
