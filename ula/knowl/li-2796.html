Item 4.2.6.5.c.

It is possible to find a basis for \(\real^3\) consisting of eignevectors because one eigenvalue \(\lambda=1\) has multiplicity one while the other \(\lambda=-1\) has multiplicity two and a two-dimensional eigenspace. A basis is

\begin{equation*} \left\{\threevec{-2}32, \threevec10{-1}, \threevec012\right\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
