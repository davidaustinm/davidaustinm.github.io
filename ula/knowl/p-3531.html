First, we compute

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} A\vvec_1 = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 0.8 \amp 0.6 \\ 0.2 \amp 0.4 \\ \end{array}\right] \twovec{3}{1} \amp {}={} \twovec{3}{1} = 1\vvec_1 \\ \\ A\vvec_2 = \left[\begin{array}{rr} 0.8 \amp 0.6 \\ 0.2 \amp 0.4 \\ \end{array}\right] \twovec{-1}{1} \amp {}={} \twovec{-0.2}{0.2} = 0.2\vvec_2\text{.} \\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

This shows that \(\vvec_1\) is an eigenvector of \(A\) with eigenvalue \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\vvec_2\) is an eigenvector of \(A\) with eigenvalue \(\lambda_2=0.2\text{.}\)
