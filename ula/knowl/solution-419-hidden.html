1. \(A\) is \(5\times4\text{,}\) \(U\) is \(5\times5\text{,}\) and \(V\) is \(4\times4\text{.}\)

  3. The first three columns, \(\uvec_1\text{,}\) \(\uvec_2\text{,}\) and \(\uvec_3\text{,}\) of \(U\) form an orthonormal basis for \(\col(A)\text{.}\)

  4. The last column \(\vvec_4\) of \(V\) is a basis for \(\nul(A)\text{.}\)

  5. \(Q^TQ=I\) since each entry is a dot product of two vectors in an orthonormal set.

  6. \(QQ^T\) projects vectors orthogonally onto \(\col(A)\text{.}\)
