Solution 4.4.5.9.1.

  1. If \(A=\mattwo{\frac12}{\frac12}10\text{,}\) then we have \(\xvec_{n+1} = A\xvec_n\text{.}\)

  2. The eigenvalues are \(\lambda_1=1\) and \(\lambda_2=-\frac12\) with associated eigenvectors \(\vvec_1=\twovec11\) and \(\vvec_2=\twovec{1}{-2}\text{.}\)

  3. We have one eigenvalue \(|\lambda_1| = 1\text{,}\) which means this system does not fall into any of our cases.

  4. After constructing an augmented matrix and row reducing, we find \(\xvec_0 = \frac23\vvec_1 + \frac13\vvec_2\text{.}\)

  5. We have \(\xvec_n=\frac23\vvec_1+ \frac13\left(-\frac12\right)^n\vvec_n\text{.}\)

  6. As \(n\) becomes large, the coefficient \(\left(-\frac12\right)^n\) becomes very small. Therefore, \(\xvec_n\approx \frac23\vvec_1 = \twovec{\frac23}{\frac23}\text{.}\) This shows that \(G_n\) stabilizes at \(\frac23\text{.}\)
