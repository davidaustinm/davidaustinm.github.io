Solution 3.5.5.1.1.

  1. \(\nul(A)\) is a subspace of \(\real^6\) and \(\col(A)\) is a subspace of \(\real^4\text{.}\)

  2. Becuase there are three pivot positions, we see that \(\rank(A) = 3\text{.}\) Therefore, \(\dim~\nul(A) = 6-3=3\) and \(\dim~\col(A)=3\text{.}\)

  3. A basis for \(\col(A)\) is given by the columns of \(A\) that contain pivot positions. Therefore, a basis is

    \begin{equation*} \fourvec{2}{-4}{6}{4}, \fourvec{0}{-1}{0}{-1}, \fourvec{0}{0}{3}{0}\text{.} \end{equation*}

  4. We can write a parametric description for the solution space to the homogeneous equation as

    \begin{equation*} \xvec = \left[\begin{array}{r} x_1 \\ 2x_4 - 3x_6 \\ -x_4-4x_6 \\ x_4 \\ x_6 \\ x_6 \end{array}\right] = x_1 \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{array}\right] +x_4 \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{array}\right] + x_6 \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

    Therefore, a basis for \(\nul(A)\) is

    \begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{r} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{array}\right], \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \end{array}\right], \left[\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ -3 \\ -4 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
