Item c.

There is a real eigenvalue \(\lambda_1=1.058\text{,}\) which is larger than \(1\text{.}\) The other eigenvalues are complex \(\lambda_{2,3} = -0.128\pm 0.506i\) and satisfy \(|\lambda_{2,3}| \lt 1\text{.}\) Therefore, the complex eigenvalues will pull vectors in toward the line defined by the eigenvector \(\vvec_1\text{.}\) After a long time, the population is \(\xvec_k\approx c_1(1.058)^k\vvec_1\text{.}\) All the populations grow annually by a factor of \(1.058\) or 5.8%, and there are approximately 38 juveniles and 29 yearlings for every 100 adults.
