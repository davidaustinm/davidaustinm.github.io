Item 1.3.5.5.a.

We will equate the number of hydrogen and oxygen atoms before and after the reaction.

  1. Before the reaction, there are two hydrogen atoms for every hydrogen molecule. Since there are \(x\) hydrogen molecules, we have \(2x\) hydrogen atoms before the reaction.

    After the reaction, there are two hydrogen atoms for every water molecule. Since there are \(z\) hydrogen molecules, we have \(2z\) hydrogen atoms after the reaction. This gives the equation \(2x=2z\text{.}\)

  2. Similarly, there are \(2y\) oxygen atoms before the reaction and \(z\) oxygen atoms after. This gives the equation \(2y = z\text{.}\)

  3. We have the linear system

    \begin{equation*} \begin{alignedat}{4} 2x \amp \amp \amp {}-{} \amp 2z \amp {}={} \amp 0 \\ \amp \amp 2y \amp {}-{} \amp z \amp {}={} \amp 0\text{.} \\ \end{alignedat} \end{equation*}

    When we create the augmented matrix and find its reduced row echelon form, we see that \(z\) is a free variable so that

    \begin{equation*} \begin{alignedat}{2} x \amp {}={} \amp z \\ y \amp {}={} \amp \frac12 z\text{.} \\ \end{alignedat} \end{equation*}

    We need all the quantities to be integers, and the smallest value of \(z\) that makes \(y\) an integer is \(z=2\text{.}\) Therefore, we have \(x=2\text{,}\) \(y=1\text{,}\) and \(z=2\text{.}\) The reaction is then

    \begin{equation*} 2\thinspace \text{H}_2 + \thinspace\text{O}_2 \to 2\thinspace \text{H}_2\text{O}\text{.} \end{equation*}
