Solution 4.5.1.1.

  1. Expressing the set of equations in matrix form, we see that \(A = \mattwo{0.8}{0.4}{0.2}{0.6}\text{.}\)

  2. We have eigenvalues \(\lambda_1 = 1\) and \(\lambda_2 = 0.4\) with associated eigenvectors \(\vvec_1 = \twovec21\) and \(\vvec_2=\twovec{-1}{1}\text{.}\)

  3. We find that \(\xvec_0=\twovec{1500}{0} = 500\vvec_1-500\vvec_2\text{.}\)

  4. Multiplying by the matrix \(A\) has the effect of multiplying the eigenvectors by their associated eigenvalues. Therefore, \(\xvec_k=500\vvec_1-500(0.4)^k\vvec_2\text{.}\)

  5. As \(k\) becomes large, \(0.4^k\) becomes very close to zero. Therefore \(\xvec_k\approx500\vvec_1 = \twovec{1000}{500}\text{.}\) This tells us that \(1000\) cars are at location \(P\) and \(500\) are at \(Q\text{.}\)
