Solution 3.2.5.5.1.

  1. By forming the matrix \(C_{\bcal}=\left[\begin{array}{rrr} \vvec_1 \amp \vvec_2 \amp \vvec_3 \end{array}\right]\) and finding its reduced row echelon form, we see that the vectors are linearly independent and span \(\real^3\text{.}\) They therefore form a basis of \(\real^3\text{.}\)

  2. We solve \(C_{\bcal}\coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \xvec\) to find \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal} = \threevec{15}{0}{0}\text{.}\)

  3. We have

    \begin{equation*} C_{\bcal}= \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \amp \frac{1}{2} \\ 1 \amp 0 \amp -1 \\ 1 \amp -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \amp \frac{1}{2} \end{array}\right], C_{\bcal}^{-1}= \left[\begin{array}{rrr} \frac{1}{3} \amp \frac{1}{3} \amp \frac{1}{3} \\ \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \amp 0 \amp -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} \amp -\frac{2}{3} \amp \frac{1}{3} \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

    Since we have \(\coords{\xvec}{\bcal}=C_{\bcal}^{-1}\xvec\text{,}\) we have \(c_1=\frac13 x_1 + \frac13 x_2 + \frac13 x_3 = \frac13(x_1+x_2+x_3) \text{.}\)
