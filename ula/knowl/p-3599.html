  1. Every two-dimensional vector is an eigenvector with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda=3\text{.}\)

  2. We have eigenvectors \(\vvec_1=\twovec{1}{0}\) with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda_1=-2\) and \(\vvec_2=\twovec{0}{1}\) with \(\lambda_2=4\text{.}\)

  3. Every vector is an eigenvector of the identity matrix with associated eigenvalue \(\lambda=1\text{.}\)

  4. The standard basis vectors \(\evec_j\) are eigenvectors and the associated eigenvalues are the corresponding diagonal entries.
