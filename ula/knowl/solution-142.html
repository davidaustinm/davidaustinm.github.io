Solution 3.3.4.4.1.

  1. We find that \(\coords{\xvec}{\wcal} = \left[\begin{array}{c} 165 \\ 13.2 \\ -2.5 \\ 13.0 \\ \end{array}\right] \text{.}\)

  2. We have the approximation \(\coords{\yvec}{\wcal} = \left[\begin{array}{c} 165 \\ 13.2 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]\) and \(\yvec=\left[\begin{array}{c} 178.2 \\ 178.2 \\ 151.8 \\ 151.8 \\ \end{array}\right] \text{.}\)

  3. The first two components are equal as are the last two components. Since we see no difference in these components, we have lost the information that differentiates the components on a small scale.

  4. We find the wavelet coefficients

    \begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 167 \amp -0.7 \amp -1.0 \amp 1.9 \\ 6.4 \amp 3.8 \amp 3.0 \amp 0.4 \\ -2.1 \amp -1.4 \amp 1.0 \amp -1.8 \\ 5.8 \amp 5.8 \amp 1.5 \amp 2.5 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

  5. We find the approximate luminance values to be

    \begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{rrrr} 177 \amp 177 \amp 170 \amp 170 \\ 177 \amp 177 \amp 170 \amp 170 \\ 156 \amp 156 \amp 165 \amp 165 \\ 156 \amp 156 \amp 165 \amp 165 \\ \end{array}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}

  6. The luminance values are constant on \(2\times2\) blocks. It is as if the image is represented with pixels that are twice as wide and twice as tall.
