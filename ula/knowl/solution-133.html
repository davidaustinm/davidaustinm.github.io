Solution 3.2.5.7.1.

  1. Yes. A matrix formed from \(m\) linearly independent vectors in \(\real^m\) will have a pivot position in every column. Since the matrix has the same number of rows and columns, there must also be a pivot position in every row. This means that the vectors span \(\real^m\) and therefore form a basis.

  2. Yes. An invertible matrix is row equivalent to the identity matrix, which means that the columns are linearly independent and span \(\real^m\text{.}\) This implies that the columns form a basis of \(\real^m\text{.}\)

  3. Yes. The matrix \(A\) is row equivalent to the identity matrix so \(B\) must be as well. This means that the columns of \(B\) form a basis for \(\real^m\text{.}\)
