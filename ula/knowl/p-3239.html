The linearity of matrix multiplication, expressed in PropositionÂ 2.2.3, tells us that \(\nul(A)\) is a subspace of \(\real^n\text{.}\) If \(\xvec_1\) and \(\xvec_2\) are both vectors in \(\nul(A)\text{,}\) we know that \(A\xvec_1 = \zerovec\) and \(A\xvec_2 = \zerovec\text{.}\) A linear combination of \(\xvec_1\) and \(\xvec_2\) can be written as \(c_1\xvec_1 + c_2\xvec_2\text{.}\) This linear combination is in \(\nul(A)\) because

\begin{equation*} A(c_1\xvec_1 + c_2\xvec_2) = c_1A\xvec_1 + c_2A\xvec_2 = c_1\zerovec + c_2\zerovec = \zerovec\text{.} \end{equation*}
