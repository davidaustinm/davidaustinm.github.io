Accessibility

  • Student Accessibilty Office contacted the GVSU math department in the summer of 2023. A visually-impaired student would be enrolled in our precalculus class in Fall 2023.

  • The textbook publisher wanted $40,000 for a braille edition of the textbook.

  • Rob Beezer converted Matt Boelkins’ open precalculus textbook Active Prelude to Calculus from PreTeXt source into braille.

  • I recreated all the diagrams in PreFigure and embossed tactile versions.

  • Total cost: $0

Accessibility

Why should we pay attention to accessibility?

  • We all want to support all our students.

  • It’s the law.

  • It will help you become a better author.

What is PreFigure?

PreFigure is to figures what PreTeXt is to text.

  • PreFigure is a PreTeXt-like way to create accessible mathematical diagrams.

  • Write once, read everywhere. From the same source, PreFigure creates

    • SVG for HTML, including annotations

    • PDF for print,

    • tactile graphics for embossing,

    • more ….

  • Fully integrated into PreTeXt

Anatomy of a PreFigure diagram

<diagram dimensions="(300,300)" margins="5">
    <definition>a=1</definition>
    <definition>f(x)=exp(x/3)*cos(x)</definition>

    <coordinates bbox="[-4,-4,4,4]">
      
        <grid-axes xlabel="x" ylabel="y"/>
        <graph function="f" />
        <tangent-line function="f" point="a"/>
        <point p="(a, f(a))">
            <m>(a,f(a))</m>
        </point>
        
    </coordinates>
</diagram>
